# Principles

> The founding principles. Short. Each has an owner and an enforcement mechanism
> (or `pending`). Advisory by nature; the enforceable cut lives in `governance/` + code.

## 1. Bound to be free

A thing can only be governed if it has a **governor** and a **jurisdiction**. The
boundary is **not a cage — it is the precondition for freedom and trust.** Draw the
limits, then say: *"do whatever you do best — solve, create, explore — but don't
break these rules or exceed these bounds."* Maximum latitude inside; hard edges at
the perimeter. That is what lets the governor trust the system and leave it alone.

*v1 failed the opposite way: unbound → sprawled → ungovernable.* The point was never
to restrict the model's ability; it was to make that ability **safe to unleash.**

- **Enforcement:** the boundary (`doctor`) · write-zones (write-fence) · the gates.
- **Owner:** `ov` / Jeremy.

## 2. Observability is the precondition for governance

You cannot govern what you cannot see. You will **not** see everything — but
**nothing may be silently dropped.** Every input reaches a terminal outcome or the
reconciler raises it; every action is traceable; the "I don't know" pile is never
hidden. More observable → more governable → more repeatable. (A government doesn't
watch every street — it ensures nothing important goes unrecorded.)

- **Enforcement:** the closed loop + reconciler · four-tuple provenance · the `doctor`.
- **Owner:** `ov`.
